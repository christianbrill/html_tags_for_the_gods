The <blockquote> tag

Whenever there is need for a block quotation on your website, then the <blockquote> tag is your best friend. It does exactly what the name suggests: create a block quotation.

<blockquote>
   The greatest fear of man is lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Eaque quae, omnis, animi nam facere sit officiis repellat error accusantium quia a debitis tempora ipsa corrupti illum. Voluptatem sint illum beatae. Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Obcaecati, mollitia nesciunt earum molestiae, impedit ipsum similique! Labore rerum reprehenderit nobis beatae cum, maxime soluta, mollitia cupiditate ipsum consequatur possimus voluptas.
</blockquote>

On your website, the block quotation will be rendered like so:

The greatest fear of man is lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Eaque quae, omnis, animi nam facere sit officiis repellat error accusantium quia a debitis tempora ipsa corrupti illum. Voluptatem sint illum beatae. Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Obcaecati, mollitia nesciunt earum molestiae, impedit ipsum similique! Labore rerum reprehenderit nobis beatae cum, maxime soluta, mollitia cupiditate ipsum consequatur possimus voluptas.

As you can see, by default, your browser will render the block quotation as quite bleak. However, with a little help from our friend CSS, we can change this quickly:

blockquote {
   width: 50%;
   text-align: justify;
}

blockquote:before,
blockquote:after {
   content: '"';
}

Doing this will add the missing quotation marks and add a little bit of styling to your quotation. Of course, you can style it whichever way you want.
